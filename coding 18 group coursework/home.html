Baltic's biggest nerdcon and tech-fiesta!

Cosplay! Music! Esports! You name it, we've got it!

DreamHack 2018 will bring together over 2000 cosplay artists from all over Europe. Whether you're a newcomer to cosplay scene, or hardcore veteran of many moons, we welcome you to join the contests or just show-off your majestic dress-up skills. We're expecting extremely spicy competition this time though, as some most famous cosplay artists are expected to show up and raise the some hell, among them such wonders of new world as Yaya Han, Jessica Nigri and Misa Chang, as well as glamurous Reikia, and even the legendary Bianca Beauchamp, debuting with her super-secret and (allegedly) super-shiny costume collection.
If you don't want to miss this historic gathering of latex, plastic, LED lights and giant mecha swords, hurry and sign up for the contest, as the participation is completely free. The non-competetive cosplay events required no sign up, but you wont get the prizes either, so we strongly encourage you to enter the contests and show-off your mastery with needle, glue gun or CNC printer for the chance to win awesome prizes and be a part of this historic occasion!
Deadline for competetive events sign up is August 20th, so if you're planning to show everyone what kind of dough you're made of, make sure you sign up before that, or you won't be able to compete with the best of them. Also the shiny of em.

At tech conference representatives from IBM and Intel will demonstrate their latest achievements in the new and fascinating technological sphere - light based computing. Compared to what these companies are working on, good old fiber-optic cable seems like ancient history, so don't miss this potentialy future-changing tech demo, what you will see in the conference might soon become a mass produced high-performance reality, and whether you'll be on the bleeding edge of progress or drag behind everyone else, is only up to you!
Live tech demo of the technology will demonstrate first efficiently working prototype of central processing unit, as well as possible setups for such system in home conditions, and, of course, the capabilities of such system for both gaming and scientific purposes! You won't want to miss this one!
